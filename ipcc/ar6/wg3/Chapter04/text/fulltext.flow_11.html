4.2.2.2
Methods to project emissions under NDCs and current policies
26
A variety of different methods are used to assess emissions implications of NDCs and current policies
27
over the time horizon to 2025 or 2030. Some of these projections were explicitly submitted as part of
28
an official communication to UNFCCC (e.g., Biennial Report, Biennial Update Reports or National
29
Communications) while the majority is from independent studies.
30
Methods that are used in independent studies (but that can also underlie the official communications)
31
can broadly be separated into two groups,
32
(i)
system modelling studies which analyse policies and targets in a comprehensive modelling
33
framework such an integrated assessment, energy systems or integrated land-use model to
34
project emissions (or other indicators) of mitigation targets in NDCs and current policies,
35
either at the national or global scale (noting some differences in the systems), and
36
(ii)
hybrid approaches that typically start out with emissions pathways as assessed by other
37
published studies (e.g., the IEA World Energy Outlook, national emissions pathways such
38
as those specified in some NDCs) and use these directly or apply additional modifications
39
to them.
40
System modelling studies are conducted at global, regional and national scales. Global models provide
41
an overview, are necessary for assessment of global phenomena (e.g., temperature change), can
42
integrate climate models and trade effects. National models typically include more details on sectors,
43
technology, behaviour and intersectoral linkages, but often use simplifying assumptions for
44
international trade (e.g., the Armington elasticity approach). Critically, they can also better reflect local
45
