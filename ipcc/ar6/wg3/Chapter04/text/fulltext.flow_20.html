4.2.2.6
Tracking progress in implementing and achieving NDCs
20
Under the Enhanced Transparency Framework, countries will transition from reporting biennial reports
21
(BRs) and biennial update reports (BURs) to reporting biennial transparency reports (BTRs) starting,
22
at the latest, by December 2024. Each Party will be required to report information necessary to track
23
progress made in implementing and achieving its NDC under the Paris Agreement (UNFCCC 2018b).
24
Thus, no official data exists yet on tracking progress of individual NDCs.
25
Meanwhile, there is some literature at global and national level that aims at assessing whether countries
26
are on track or progressing towards implementing their NDCs and to which degree the NDCs
27
collectively are sufficient to reach the temperature targets of the Paris agreement (Quéré et al. 2018;
28
Höhne et al. 2018; Roelfsema et al. 2020; Rogelj et al. 2016; den Elzen et al. 2019; Höhne et al. 2020).
29
Most of these studies focus on major emitters such as G20 countries and with the aim to inform countries
30
to strengthen their ambition regularly, e.g., through progress of NDCs and as part of the global stocktake
31
(Höhne et al. 2018; Peters et al. 2017). However, a limited number of studies assess the implementation
32
gaps of conditional NDCs in terms of finance, technology and capacity building support. Some authors
33
conclude that finance needed to fulfil conditional NDCs exceeds available resources or the current long-
34
term goal for finance (USD100 billion yr
-1
) (Pauw et al. 2019); others assess financial resources needed
35
for forest-related activities (Kissinger et al. 2019) (15.4.2). The literature suggests that consistent and
36
harmonised approach to track progress of countries towards their NDCs would be helpful (den Elzen et
37
al. 2019; Höhne et al. 2018; Peters et al. 2017), and negotiations on a common tabular format are
38
expected to conclude during COP26 in November 2021.
