4.2.2.4
Estimated impact of COVID-19 and governmental responses on emissions projections
1
The impacts of COVID-19 and national governments’ economic recovery measures on current (see
2
2.2.2) and projected emissions of individual countries and globally under current policies scenarios until
3
2030 may be significant, although estimates are highly uncertain and vary across the few available
4
studies. The analyses published to date (October 2021) are based on limited information about how
5
COVID-19 has affected the economy and hence GHG emissions across countries so far in 2020, and
6
also based on assumptions about COVID-19’s longer term impact. Moreover, the comparison of pre-
7
and post-COVID-19 projections captures the impact of COVID-19 as well as other factors such as the
8
consideration of recently adopted policies not related to COVID-19, as well as methodological changes.
9

10
2020a; Climate Action Tracker 2020; Keramidas et al. 2021; Dafnomilis et al. 2020), the impact of the
11
general slowdown of the economy due to the COVID-19 pandemic and its associated policy responses
12
would lead to a reduced estimate of global GHG emissions in 2030 of about 1 to 5 GtCO
2
eq, equivalent
13

14

15

17

18

19

21
