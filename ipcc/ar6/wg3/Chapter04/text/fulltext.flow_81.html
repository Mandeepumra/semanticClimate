17

18
2021; Michaelowa et al. 2018). Likewise, it is not expected that spontaneous behaviour change or
19

described in 4.2.5, or in the shifts in development pathways described in 4.3.3. On the contrary, stringent

22

Policy integration, addressing multiple objectives, is an essential component of shifting development
25
pathways and accelerating mitigation (robust evidence, high agreement). A shift in development
26
pathways that fosters accelerated mitigation may best be achieved through integrated actions that
27
comprise policies in support of the broader SDG agenda, based on country-specific priorities (4.3.2,
28
13.8, 13.9). These may include for example, fiscal policies, or integrating industrial (Nilsson et al. 2021)
29
and energy policies (Fragkos et al. 2021) with climate policies. Similarly, sectoral transitions that aspire
30
to shifting development pathways towards sustainability often have multiple objectives, and deploy a
31
diverse mix or package of policies and institutional measures (Cross-Chapter Box 5).

34

35
