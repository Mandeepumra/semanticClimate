4.2.1
Mitigation targets and measures in nationally determined contributions 26
A central instrument of the Paris Agreement is the NDCs, submitted by each country, and reflecting
27
national efforts to reduce GHG emissions and build resilience to the impacts of climate change. Every
28
five years, collective progress will be compared against long-term goals of the Paris Agreement.
29
Considering the outcome of a global stocktake, countries will prepare subsequent NDCs, showing
30
progression in their ambition and enhancing international cooperation (UNFCCC 2015a).
31
Prior to COP21, in 2015, most countries submitted their INDCs (Intended Nationally Determined
32
Contributions), which included mitigation targets for 2025 or 2030. INDCs become first NDCs on
33
ratification and/or after national governments’ revision, and by 11 October 2021, the official NDC
34
registry contained 194 first NDCs with 105 new and updated NDCs from 132 Parties to the Paris
35
Agreement, covering 53% of the total global emissions in 2019 of 52.4 GtCO
2
-eq without LULUCF,
36
and 13 second NDCs. Most of the Parties that submitted new or updated NDCs have demonstrated
37
increased ambition in addressing climate change. Moreover, though some countries like China have not
38
submitted their updated NDCs yet, they have already announced their updated NDC goals somewhere.
39
Countries will take the first stock in 2023 based on their progression towards achieving the objectives
40
of Paris Agreement (UNFCCC 2015a, 2018a; SB Chairs 2021) (14.3.2.5).
41
