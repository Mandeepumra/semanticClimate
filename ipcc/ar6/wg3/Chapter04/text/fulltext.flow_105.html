FAQ 4.2 Option 1: What is to be done in the near-term to accelerate mitigation and shift
24
development pathways?
25
Increasing speed of implementation, breadth of action across all sectors of the economy, and depth of
26
emission reduction faces important obstacles, that are rooted in the underlying structure of societies
27
(4.2.7). Addressing these obstacles amounts to shifting away from existing developmental trends (i.e.,
28
shifting development pathways, Cross-Chapter Box 5). This can be done by strengthening governance
29
and institutional capacity, aligning technology and innovation systems with low-carbon development,
30
facilitating behaviour change and providing adequate finance within the context of multi-objective
31
policy packages and sequences (4.4.1). Shifting development pathways towards sustainability broadens
32
the scope for, and is thus a complement to, accelerated mitigation (4.3).
